## Executive Summary

This PRD outlines the development of a next-generation to-do list mobile application designed to help users overcome task management fragmentation, procrastination, and lack of productivity insights. Unlike traditional to-do apps, our solution combines intelligent task prioritization, AI-powered task breakdown, and gamification to create a motivating, habit-forming experience.

**Target Launch:** MVP in 6 months (iOS & Android)  
**Primary Goal:** Achieve 100K+ DAU within 12 months with 65%+ task completion rate  
**Key Differentiator:** AI-assisted productivity with psychological motivation design

## 1. Product Overview

### 1.1 Problem Statement

Modern knowledge workers and students face critical productivity challenges:

- **Task Overwhelm:** Users create long, unstructured task lists that become anxiety-inducing rather than helpful
- **Poor Prioritization:** 67% of users struggle to identify what to work on next (internal research)
- **Context Switching:** Average user switches between 3-4 productivity apps daily, losing 23 minutes/day to context switching
- **Lack of Motivation:** Traditional to-do apps feel like chores, leading to 42% abandonment within 30 days
- **No Actionable Insights:** Users don't understand their productivity patterns or improvement opportunities

### 1.2 Vision & Objectives

**Vision:** Become the most intelligent and motivating task management companion that helps users not just track tasks, but actually complete them and build better habits.

**Primary Objectives:**
- Increase user task completion rate by 40% vs. traditional to-do apps
- Reduce time spent on task management from 15 min/day to 5 min/day
- Achieve 70%+ 30-day retention rate
- Build a scalable platform supporting individual and team productivity

### 1.3 Value Proposition

**For Individual Users:**
- AI breaks down overwhelming tasks into manageable steps automatically
- Smart prioritization tells you exactly what to do next
- Gamification and streaks make productivity rewarding
- Deep analytics reveal your productivity patterns

**For Team Users:**
- Shared task lists with real-time collaboration
- Team progress visibility without micromanagement
- Integration with existing workflows

**Competitive Advantage:**
- AI-powered task intelligence (not just a digital list)
- Psychology-based motivation system
- Offline-first architecture with seamless sync
- Privacy-focused (no ads, ethical data use)

## 4. Functional Requirements

### 4.1 Task Creation Flow

**Trigger Points:**
- Tap "+" FAB button (primary)
- Voice command "Add task [task name]"
- Share extension from other apps
- Quick add from notification/widget

**User Flow:**
1. User taps "+" button
2. Keyboard appears with focus on task title field
3. User types task title (required)
4. User optionally taps to expand details:
   - Description (text area, 500 char limit)
   - Due date (date picker)
   - Priority (3 buttons: High/Medium/Low, default: Medium)
   - Category/Tags (multi-select dropdown)
   - Subtasks (add/remove)
5. If task contains complexity keywords â†’ AI suggests "Break down this task?"
6. User taps "Create" â†’ Task appears in appropriate list with animation
7. If due date set â†’ System schedules reminder notification

**Validation Rules:**
- Task title: Required, 1-200 characters
- Description: Optional, max 500 characters
- Due date: Cannot be in the past
- Subtasks: Max 20 per task

**AI Enhancement Point:**
- On task creation, AI analyzes title + description
- If detected as complex (>3 actions, project-like keywords), prompt AI breakdown
- Show suggested subtasks in modal with Edit/Accept/Dismiss options

---

### 4.2 Task Completion Flow

**User Flow - Quick Complete:**
1. User swipes right on task OR taps checkbox
2. Satisfying animation plays (checkmark + confetti for milestone tasks)
3. Task moves to "Completed" section (collapsed by default)
4. If part of streak â†’ Streak counter increments
5. If achievement unlocked â†’ Badge notification appears
6. XP awarded (visible progress bar animation)

**User Flow - Complete with Subtasks:**
1. If task has incomplete subtasks â†’ Show confirmation: "Task has incomplete subtasks. Mark all complete?"
2. User confirms â†’ All subtasks marked complete + main task completes
3. Progress animation shows subtask completion cascade

**Undo Mechanism:**
- "Undo" toast appears for 5 seconds after completion
- User can tap to revert completion
- Automatically dismissed after timeout

---

### 4.3 Reminder & Notification System

**Reminder Types:**
1. **Time-Based:** Fire at specific date/time
2. **Location-Based:** Fire when entering/exiting geofence (500m radius)
3. **Smart Reminders:** System-suggested based on task priority + due date

**Notification Behavior:**
- **Due Today (Morning):** "You have 5 tasks due today" at 8 AM
- **Due Soon:** "Task due in 1 hour" notification
- **Overdue:** "2 tasks overdue" gentle reminder at 6 PM
- **Streak Risk:** "Complete 1 task to keep your 15-day streak" at 8 PM

**User Controls:**
- Global notification settings (enable/disable types)
- Quiet hours (no notifications during sleep)
- Notification sound customization
- Per-task reminder customization

**Technical Requirements:**
- iOS: Use UNUserNotificationCenter
- Android: WorkManager for scheduled tasks
- Handle notification permissions gracefully
- Batch notifications to prevent spam

---

### 4.4 Sync & Conflict Resolution

**Sync Triggers:**
- App foreground (check for updates)
- Task created/modified/deleted (immediate sync)
- Periodic background sync (every 15 min if changes detected)

**Conflict Resolution Strategy:**
- **Last-Write-Wins:** Most recent modification timestamp wins
- **Task Deletion:** Deleted state propagates to all devices
- **User Notification:** If major conflict detected (rare), prompt user to choose version
- **Merge Logic:** For compatible changes (different fields), merge both

**Offline Queue:**
- All offline actions stored in local queue
- Queue processed sequentially when connection restored
- Visual indicator shows pending sync count
- Manual "Force Sync" option in settings

---

### 4.5 AI Task Breakdown

**Activation Conditions:**
- User creates task with >5 words AND action verbs (write, create, build, organize)
- User explicitly taps "AI Breakdown" button in task detail
- System detects project-like language ("plan," "develop," "research")

**AI Processing Flow:**
1. Send task title + description to AI service (anonymized)
2. AI returns 3-7 suggested subtasks with order
3. Display in modal: "AI suggests breaking this down:"
4. User can:
   - Accept all (converts to subtasks)
   - Edit individual suggestions
   - Dismiss entirely
5. Track user acceptance rate for ML improvement

**AI Prompt Engineering:**
```
Task: [User's task title and description]
Context: [Task category if available]

Generate 3-7 actionable subtasks to complete this task. 
Each subtask should:
- Be a single, clear action
- Start with an action verb
- Be completable in <2 hours
- Be logically ordered

Format: JSON array of subtask titles
```

**Privacy & Transparency:**
- Clear disclosure: "AI uses your task text to generate suggestions"
- Option to disable AI features in settings
- Data not used for advertising, deleted after processing

---

### 4.6 Focus Mode

**Activation:**
- User taps "Focus" on any task
- Widget/shortcut: "Start Focus Session"

**Focus Mode UI:**
- Full-screen task view
- Large timer (default 25 min Pomodoro)
- Minimal controls: Start/Pause, Stop Session
- Hide all other tasks (reduce distraction)
- Dark background (reduce eye strain)

**Session Flow:**
1. User starts timer
2. Optional: Phone enters Do Not Disturb (with permission)
3. Timer counts down with progress ring
4. At completion: Celebration + break prompt (5 min break)
5. Log session to task (focus time tracked)
6. Award bonus XP for focused sessions

**Session Data Captured:**
- Duration
- Completion status (finished vs. abandoned)
- Time of day
- Associated task

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

### 5.1 Performance

**Response Time:**
- App launch: <2 seconds (cold start), <0.5 seconds (warm start)
- Task creation: <200ms from tap to UI update
- Sync: Complete within 3 seconds for <100 tasks
- Search results: <500ms for up to 10,000 tasks

**Scalability:**
- Support 10,000+ tasks per user without performance degradation
- Handle 100+ tasks visible in single list smoothly (60 fps scrolling)
- Sync optimized for differential updates (don't re-download everything)

**Battery & Resource Usage:**
- Background sync: <2% battery drain per 24 hours
- Location reminders: Use significant location change (not continuous GPS)
- Efficient rendering: Recycle views, lazy load large lists

**Data Usage:**
- Sync protocol: Send only deltas (changed fields)
- Image compression for any attachments (future feature)
- Target: <5 MB/month data usage for average user

---

### 5.2 Security & Privacy

**Data Protection:**
- End-to-end encryption for task content in transit (TLS 1.3)
- At-rest encryption for local database (iOS Keychain, Android EncryptedSharedPreferences)
- User data stored in isolated, encrypted cloud storage

**Authentication:**
- Multi-factor authentication (optional, recommended)
- Biometric login (Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint)
- Session management: 30-day refresh tokens
- Account recovery via email verification

**Privacy Commitments:**
- No selling user data to third parties
- No advertising based on task content
- Minimal data collection (only what's needed for functionality)
- GDPR/CCPA compliant: Right to deletion, data portability
- Anonymous analytics (no PII in analytics events)

**AI Privacy:**
- Task text sent to AI service is anonymized (no user ID attached)
- AI processing data deleted within 24 hours
- Option to disable AI features entirely
- Clear in-app disclosure of AI data usage

---

### 5.3 Scalability

**User Growth:**
- Architecture supports 1M+ users in first year
- Horizontal scaling for API servers (auto-scaling based on load)
- Database sharding strategy for >10M users

**Infrastructure:**
- Microservices architecture (task service, sync service, AI service, analytics)
- CDN for static assets
- Multi-region deployment for reduced latency

**Data Management:**
- Archive old completed tasks (>1 year) to cold storage
- User can manually trigger archive/deletion
- Efficient indexing for fast queries at scale

---

### 5.4 Accessibility

**WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance:**
- Full screen reader support (VoiceOver, TalkBack)
- Semantic labels for all interactive elements
- Minimum contrast ratio 4.5:1 for text
- Dynamic text sizing (support iOS/Android system font sizes)

**Inclusive Design:**
- Colorblind-friendly: Don't rely solely on color (use icons + color)
- Haptic feedback for important actions
- Voice control support (Siri Shortcuts, Google Assistant)
- Keyboard navigation support (for accessibility keyboards)

**Localization Readiness:**

### 8.2 Cloud Sync Strategy

**Sync Protocol:**
- RESTful API for basic operations (CRUD)
- WebSocket for real-time updates (optional, Phase 2)
- Differential sync: Only send changed fields, not entire objects

**Data Model:**
- Each task has: `id` (UUID), `user_id`, `title`, `description`, `due_date`, `priority`, `status`, `created_at`, `updated_at`, `deleted_at` (soft delete)
- Timestamp-based conflict resolution: `updated_at` determines latest version
- Sync token: Track last sync timestamp per device

**Offline-First Approach:**
- Local database is source of truth
- All operations write to local DB first (instant UI update)
- Background service syncs to cloud when online
- Conflict resolution on sync failure (prompt user if critical)

**Sync Optimization:**
- Batch API calls (sync up to 100 tasks per request)
- Compression: gzip API responses
- Incremental sync: Use `last_sync_timestamp` to fetch only new/changed tasks

---

### 8.3 Notification System

**Push Notifications:**
- iOS: Apple Push Notification Service (APNs)
- Android: Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM)

**Local Notifications:**
- Scheduled on-device for time-based reminders
- More reliable than relying on push (works offline)

**Notification Queue:**
- Backend maintains notification schedule
- Sends push at specified time OR device schedules local notification
- Handle timezone differences correctly

**User Preferences:**

- Allow granular control: Reminders, Streaks, Achievements, Marketing (opt-in only)

**Geofencing:**

- Battery-efficient: Use significant location change, not continuous GPS

## 10. Risks & Mitigation

### 10.1 Technical Risks

**Risk 1: Sync Conflicts at Scale**
- **Impact:** Data loss or inconsistent state across devices
- **Probability:** Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Robust conflict resolution algorithm (last-write-wins + user prompt for critical conflicts)
  - Extensive testing with multiple devices
  - Soft delete with recovery period
  - Sync logs for debugging

**Risk 2: AI Service Availability**
- **Impact:** AI task breakdown feature unavailable, user frustration
- **Probability:** Low-Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Implement fallback rule-based system
  - Cache AI responses for common task patterns
  - Clear user messaging when AI unavailable
  - Queue AI requests for retry
  - Make AI optional, not critical path

**Risk 3: Push Notification Delivery**
- **Impact:** Users miss reminders, reduced app value
- **Probability:** Medium (APNs/FCM have occasional issues)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Prefer local notifications for scheduled reminders
  - Use push as backup/supplement
  - Allow users to test notifications in settings
  - Monitor notification delivery rates

**Risk 4: Battery Drain from Location Services**
- **Impact:** User complaints, uninstalls
- **Probability:** Medium if not optimized
- **Mitigation:**
  - Use significant location change (not GPS polling)
  - Make location reminders opt-in
  - Monitor battery usage in analytics
  - Provide user control (on/off toggle)

---

### 10.2 User Adoption Risks

**Risk 5: Low User Engagement**
- **Impact:** Poor retention, failed product-market fit
- **Probability:** High (productivity app market is crowded)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Strong onboarding: Show value immediately (create first task in 10 seconds)
  - Gamification hooks users early (first achievement within 24 hours)
  - Push notifications for streak reminders (re-engagement)
  - A/B test features to optimize engagement
  - User research: Monthly interviews with churned users

**Risk 6: AI Suggestions Perceived as Inaccurate**
- **Impact:** Users distrust AI, disable feature
- **Probability:** Medium (AI not perfect)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Set expectations: "AI suggestions to get you started"
  - Easy to edit/dismiss suggestions
  - Track acceptance rate, iterate on prompts
  - Allow users to provide feedback on suggestions
  - Don't force AI, make it optional enhancement

**Risk 7: Privacy Concerns**
- **Impact:** Negative press, low trust, regulatory issues
- **Probability:** Low-Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Transparent privacy policy (plain language)
  - Minimal data collection (only functional data)
  - Clear in-app disclosures for AI usage
  - GDPR/CCPA compliance from Day 1
  - Optional features require opt-in (location, AI)

**Risk 8: Competitive Pressure**
- **Impact:** Losing market share to established players (Todoist, Things, TickTick)
- **Probability:** High
- **Mitigation:**
  - Focus on differentiation: AI + gamification + analytics (unique combo)
  - Superior UX: Faster, cleaner, more delightful
  - Community building: Engaged user base as moat
  - Rapid iteration: Ship features faster than competitors
  - Strategic partnerships: Integration with popular tools

---

### 10.3 Business Risks

**Risk 9: Monetization Challenges**
- **Impact:** Unsustainable business model
- **Probability:** Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Build engaged free user base first (focus on PMF)
  - Introduce freemium in Phase 2 (premium features, not paywalls)
  - Explore B2B: Team plans for companies
  - Alternative: Sponsorships, partnerships (avoid ads)

**Risk 10: Scaling Costs (AI API)**
- **Impact:** High per-user costs eat into margins
- **Probability:** Medium at scale
- **Mitigation:**
  - Cache common AI responses
  - Rate limit AI calls per user (e.g., 10/day for free users)
  - Explore cheaper AI providers or self-hosted models
  - Premium tier with unlimited AI usage

## 11. Open Questions & Assumptions

### 11.1 Open Questions

**Product Questions:**
1. **How many tasks is too many?** Should we cap tasks per user (e.g., 500 active tasks) to maintain performance?
2. **AI accuracy threshold:** What acceptance rate for AI suggestions is "good enough" to keep the feature?
3. **Gamification balance:** At what point does gamification become overwhelming or feel childish?
4. **Team collaboration depth:** Should MVP include any team features, or purely individual focus?
5. **Monetization timing:** When should we introduce premium features? (Day 1, or after X users/months?)

**Technical Questions:**
1. **Cross-platform code sharing:** Should we invest in KMM/React Native, or keep native apps separate?
2. **AI model choice:** Fine-tuned smaller model vs. GPT-4 API calls (cost vs. quality trade-off)?
3. **Real-time sync:** Is WebSocket overkill for MVP, or essential for great UX?
4. **Data residency:** Do we need region-specific data storage (EU, US, Asia) for compliance?

**UX Questions:**
1. **Onboarding length:** 3-screen tutorial vs. zero tutorial (learn by doing)?
2. **Notification frequency:** What's optimal for engagement without being annoying?
3. **Default view:** Should app open to "Today" or "All Tasks"?
